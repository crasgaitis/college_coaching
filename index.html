What I Offer

Here are my three most useful services, although I'm able to assist with all aspects of the application process (i.e. awards, asking for recommendations, etc.) even if not listed. During help sessions, you can tell me specific tasks you'd like to work on or I can take the wheel.

College search

Not sure where to study or what to study? Maybe you and your parents have different ideas for the next four years of your life. Or, maybe you know exactly what you want to do!

Regardless, deciding what universities you want to apply to can be a pretty long and arduous process. Fortunately, I'm here to simplify it for you. Based on your career aspirations, financial criteria, and social preferences, I will help build a list of universities tailored to YOU and your interests.

I will also assist with scholarship search and scholarship application writing.

Essay writing

Your personal statement and short answer responses are the soul of your application. Universities want you to use these sections to express who you are, but they're also looking for reasons why they should choose you over everyone else.

Whether you're brainstorming ideas, writing your first draft, or editing the 10th version of your responses, I am here to help. A fresh pair of eyes is always useful, especially when they belong to someone as passionate about your academic success as you are.

Extracurriculars

I am more than happy to help craft compelling descriptions of your existing extracurricular activities to highlight your achievements and make them stand out. However, I can also help you supplement your current extracurriculars with more projects or take your current projects to the next level.

Interested in medicine? I can get you access to research-grade equipment and assist in writing a scientific manuscript. Interested in programming? Let's launch your first machine learning application and build a real user base. Are you an artist or a writer? I'll find you some exciting new projects and start an interactive online portfolio.

About Me

Who am I? (aka why you should trust me to be your college coach)

Interested in UW Computer Science?

The highly competitive Computer Science major at the University of Washington (UW) has an admittance rate of ~20%. I am a direct-to-major Computer Science student at the UW myself and I have helped multiple students successfully join this program (both via appeal and normal application). I have also been awarded CSE departmental scholarships in the past (check out my name on one of the plaques in the CSE1 basement!) and I am currently a Paul G Allen Peer Adviser. I am also the President of an Allen Affiliate RSO (Registered Student Organization) called Synaptech, which is the university's neural engineering club.

I have mentored previous clients during one-on-one work sessions where we build projects that combine personal hobbies/extracurriculars with creating websites, mobile applications, and more. No prior coding experience necessary! We can usually finish projects within a couple weeks, depending on project complexity.

Interested in something else?

I have a very extensive assortment of research experience, ranging from field research in oceanography, working with rhesus macaque monkeys to study neuroscience, experimenting with large language models for NASA, or using machine learning to understand genomics -- just to name a few. Chances are, if there's something you're interested in, I might have dabbled in it at some point too! And, if not, I'll do a deep dive into current research and career opportunities post-graduation.

If you want to find out more about my academic or work history, you can check out my personal website, resume, and CV at my personal site.
